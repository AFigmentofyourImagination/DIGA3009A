Behaviour and personality:

These dragons aren't known as the Great Protector for nothing. They eat lava from volcanoes which saves entire islands from erupting into oblivion, as evidenced in Race to the Edge, Seasons 3, 4, 5. They have a protective streak over an entire island, including its people if they are kind to the dragon, which leads the dragon to protect them voluntarily against the volcano.

Features:

  • Spiky lobes and protrusions on back and tail
  • Large, round head
  • Tiny forelegs

Abilities:

  • Burrowing through the crust of a dormant volcano
  • Eating extremely large amounts of lava
  • Stopping and causing volcanic eruptions
  • Lava-proof skin
  • Heat-resistant saliva

Diet:

Lava

Habitat:

Volcanoes

Size:

6,09 m (20 ft) [unrealistic/DreamWorks official measurement]

15,35 m (50,33 ft) tall 28,49 m (93,5 ft) long [realistic/based on model sheet]

Wingspan:

9,14m (30 ft) [unrealistic/DreamWorks official measurement]

42,46 m (139,33 ft) [realistic/based on model sheet]

Weight:

1088,6 kg (2400lbs)

Fire Type:

Large-scale lava spew

Weaknesses:

  • Constant lava consumption requirement
  • Vulnerable to Dragon Root
  • Low reproductive rate. It only produces one (1) egg during their entire lifetime, reproducing asexually. If the egg is not submerged in lava, it will harden until it is like stone and the baby thus dies. Laying an egg also weakens the Eruptodon, as they lay them near the end of their lifetime.

Trivia:

  • The Eruptodon's armored physiology and bipedal locomotion are quite similar to a pangolin's.
  • The Eruptodon is very phoenix-like because of the way it lives for generations and then lays a single egg before it dies.
  • Eruptodons apparently grow very fast, as the New Protector (Race to the Edge, Season) became a fully-grown adult in only a few months. However, this could likely be a plot hole used for continuity.
  • The Eruptodon is similar to the Fire Dragon from the book How to Twist a Dragon's Tale, as it is a giant, volcano-dwelling dragon whose egg can only hatch once it is immersed in lava.
  • The Eruptodon's roars are modified big cat snarls and growls.
