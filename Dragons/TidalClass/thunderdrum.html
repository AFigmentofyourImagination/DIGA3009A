Behaviour and personality:

They form pods in a hunt or on migration, a feature similar to those of cetaceans. They are generally reclusive in nature when alone, living in sea caves and dark tide pools or in open waters. Understandably, they are "strident and assertive; the loud Thunderdrum always makes its feelings known".

Adolescents are shown to be mischievous to Vikings and dragons alike and they can be very annoying, as they don't stop roaring or mimicking other sounds. However, the adolescents will acquiesce to any adult of the same species, regardless of relationship or lack thereof.

Thunderdrums are so stubborn that they can be tough to train. They show a real parental instinct to any young, even if it is not their own.

According to Hiccup in Race to the Edge, Thunderdrums have a habit of holding grudges against their enemies.

Trivia:

  • In the original film, when Hiccup is reading the Book of Dragons, some of the runes on the Thunderdrum page give us an interesting tidbit: "Left ear slightly larger than right."
  • The design of the Thunderdrums resemble spotted eagle rays in shape and colour. They also appear to be based on sharks or baleen whales, as it has whale shark spots, a basking shark mouth, and has the shape of a spotted eagle ray (which is a relative of the shark). Its torso could possibly be based on the ridges of a rorqual's belly that expand when they feed. Additionally, a Thunderdrum's humming sounds similar to a baleen whale's singing.
  • Though their overall power is 73, Thunderdrums have proven to be extremely strong, as Thornado was able to carry Stoick on his back while pulling two ships out of their harbour, and another time, he carried the latter and Gobber without showing any signs of struggle.
  • Thornado and the other Thunderdrums that appear in the TV series are much smaller than the ones in How to Train Your Dragon 2.
  • Even though it has been stated that a Thunderdrum's sonic blast could kill a man at close range, the teens and Stoick only receive minimal damage or are just blasted away when Thornado, Bing, Bam, or Boom use their sonic blast. The Dragon Riders are also not affected when four of them use a combined sonic blast. This is most likely due to the reduced levels of violence in the TV series.
  • The roar of a Thunderdrum resembles the sound of a TIE fighter from Star Wars.
  • It is possible that Thunderdrums hear infrasound better, rather than normal sound.
