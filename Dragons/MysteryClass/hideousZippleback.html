Behaviour and personality:

Zipplebacks have a personality for each head, meaning two minds, one body. Occasionally the dragon will forget that they are one. The way they bicker is similar to how siblings bicker: arguing over petty things but attached at the hip (most sibling relationships anyway).

It comes as no surprise that Zipplebacks are social dragons and tend to form flights. If they are saved by someone, they must pay their longtime debt to show how grateful they are.

It's shown that Zipplebacks have a sense of humour time and time again with the main Zippleback in the franchise, Barf and Belch.

Trivia:

The head, neck, and tail movements were inspired by cobras. Posture and walking structure resembles a Komodo dragon or a horned lizard.

The Zippleback is described to have 4,769 keyable animation controls, whereas humans only had 1,100.

Each Zippleback differs on which head spews gas and which ignites it.

The Hideous Zippleback resembles Zmey Gorynych, the Slavic Dragon.

Name is possibly derived from "Zippo", a brand of lighter, which is fitting. Another possibility is it refers to "zipper" as the dragon can link and unlink the spines on their necks and tails together.

Cousins of the Seashockers , and therefore it is possible they share a common two-headed ancestry.
