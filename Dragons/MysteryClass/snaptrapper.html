Behaviour and personality:

Are said to love rain, frolicking in mud whenever storms break. They are beautiful, peaceful and calm at first sight, and these dragons are also fierce and aggressive. A dangerous combination with their incredibly patient nature.

Trivia:

  • Based off a Venus Flytrap, a Hydra from Greek Mythology, and (to a lesser extent) a European dragon.
  • In the Book of Dragons short, the Snaptrapper is said to use the smell of chocolate to lure its prey. However, chocolate was unknown in Viking times, due to it being native to the Americas (North and South). It would have been more realistic to use honey, which was known to Vikings and is made from the nectar of flowers, which the Snaptrapper's heads resemble. The use of chocolate might be a reference to the books though, due to one of the villains trying to find America.
  • The Snaptrapper is the only dragon from the Book of Dragons short to never make a physical to the films or shows.
