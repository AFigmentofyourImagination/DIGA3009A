Behaviour and personality:

Cruel and cunning, this dragon raids other dragons' nests to consume their eggs and hatchlings.

They are exceptionally smart and deceiving, able to outsmart opponents, relentless. They even go so far as to pushing other dragons out of their nests to gain a place to stay temporarily before repeating its vicious process.

Trivia:

Due to its collapsible skeleton, it is one of the most flexible species of dragons.

The Cavern Crasher's ability to squeeze its body through tight spaces is similar to that of the Riproarer and Raptortongue from the books. It might also be inspired by the myths of rats and mice possessing collapsible skeletons.

The Cavern Crasher appears to be heavily inspired by fire salamanders or tiger salamanders. Fire salamanders are amphibians with yellow and black markings similar to the dragon. The Cavern Crasher's ability to produce flammable mucus may be based on the myth that the coat of mucus on the body of fire salamanders protected them from being harmed by fire.

The Cavern Crasher's knobby bumps on its back that ooze flammable mucus are reminiscent of poison glands found on the skin of many amphibians, as well as the egg pouches of Surinam toads.

The Cavern Crasher's ability to fling flammable mucus may have been influenced by the northern spiny-tailed gecko's ability to squirt a noxious fluid from its tail, and this fluid can become very flammable when mixed with ammonia.

The Cavern Crasher's behaviour and abilities could also be inspired by that of the devil's coach horse beetle, as both species can excrete fluids from certain parts of their bodies, and are both known to raid nests of other animals.
