About

Creator

I am a student who made this website for my finals in 2025. This is purely for a hyperfixation I have had ever since I can remember, I cannot remember my life before this franchise invaded my brain and set up shop in there. An important thing I told myself during this whole process is that it is ok to mess up a little, because I am human, and I am learning. I don't need to know everything right now.

History

For those who are curious, here is the process, and the decisions, and my thoughts throughout making this website. This section is mainly for me to vent, and so anyone who reads it sees that a lot of emotion and energy went into this project, maybe even soothe someone who is also spiralling during their finals and see that you do make it out on the other side.

Original summary for the index

A Book of Dragons, but on the Internet. You might say it is a Website of Dragons.

Some may say that this is what the Fandom Wiki is for, but I disagree. Most fans, myself included, do not like that DreamWorks tried to run the HTTYD franchise dry after it concluded in 2019 with HTTYD 3. This website is in honour of how I grew up knowing and loving this franchise. Before DreamWorks took over, there was a beautiful website that was my biggest inspiration for making this one, and that website was the Dragonpedia.

What happened to Dragonpedia?

Long story short, DreamWorks shut it down and merged the barebones of it into their main website that is borderline impossible to navigate. Many of the sections and features were eliminated. Things like the Dragonpedia. I have searched high and low for what happened and I still can't say. It's like they wanted it to disappear...

My thoughts

I know that the official site had a maximum of 23 dragon entries and there are 61 dragons spotted and/or mentioned in the main franchise. But it feels wrong to me to not have that kind of website without needing to needle through information on what is known and what is brought up in the games or the post HTTYD 3 era. There is another site that I found during my research, a hidden gem that I wanted to take inspiration from.

A brilliant website that inspired me: httyd-unofficial

API and Feedback

I have searched and I could not find a single thing that would give me an answer as to how to do this. Everything I have tried either failed or was too confusing for me to understand. What I have now is hopefully correct. At the time of writing this part of my thoughts, it is the 6th, and I am so burnt out, I want to cry in my mother's arms until everything is ok again.

Ok, I have figured it out, I was spiralling. To be fair to me, all of my Google searches didn't have what I needed. My feedback system now has a Google spreadsheet where it stores any submissions I get. Currently, only I can read them, if the person who is marking my project needs access to it to verify that it works, I put my email in the GitHub notes for the commit of the final feedback system. It would be under the "edited and feedback" comments.

GSAP

Honestly, I still think some of the GSAP animations we are required to add are, for lack of a better term, unnecessary. Yes, it is important for us as students to learn how to use all the tools, but I think part of learning is that knowing when it is not needed for a good website.

Despite my opinions, I added them with the limited time I had left. Let me be clear, I did not leave it until the last second, but I got overwhelmed and it lead to burn out and mental fog. In order to be kind to myself, I kept them simple as I did not want to overwhelm the user. Because it needed to be across all the pages, I decided to add the GSAP timeline to the navigation (it seemed pretty), only let some paragraphs and lists be affected by the ScrollTrigger, and have the logo spin when loading into the page (very cute). On each page, there are 4 or more elements that uses GSAP, including this paragraph.

Checking the requirements

I was tiredly reading through what I still had left to do when I saw that my API needed to be linked to my filter system. Queue the panic! (Also why is queue spelt like that?) I was ready to run to my mom at 4 in the morning to cry. I couldn't afford to fail to meet any requirements, no matter how tired I was. The first thought running through me was, "Why do they need to be linked? What do they mean the two are connected? Does that mean my feedback API means nothing?!"

What I have decided, because I still need to complete all the dragon entries, that I should leave it. My lecturers can see I did add an API, and a filter system. In my website, I do not see how the filter system and API can be linked. This is where I pessimistically believe I will lose marks. I can only hope and pray it doesn't sink my grade.

Things I would add or change if I had more time / experience.

I would definitely add some small section where the terms of some things, like Dragon Root, are explained or a pop-up to explain what it is if I had more time. I would also have made it a bit more fancy in the design. With making this website, I have learned that I might have bitten off more than I can chew in regards to the content itself. But I wanted to showcase something I love. Maybe for the next website I make, I'd go for something a bit easier.
